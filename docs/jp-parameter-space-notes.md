# JP Parameter Space — Photon-Orbit Transition at a = 0.9

Findings from three investigations kicked off during Phase 3A gate 6,
when the reference photon-orbit table at `(a=0.9, ε₃=+0.5)` and
`(a=0.9, ε₃=+1.0)` produced sub-horizon roots instead of a smooth
Kerr-continuation orbit. The prograde-column discovery is real
physics, not a script bug.

Companion to `docs/phase-3-plan.md` and
`docs/phase-3a-gates-5-6.md`. Drives the pair-selection revision for
gate 6 and the sweep-range revision for Phase 3C.

---

## 1. Executive summary

- The Kerr-continuation prograde equatorial photon orbit in the JP
  metric (`h(r,θ) = ε₃ M³ r / Σ²`, Johannsen 2013 form) merges with
  the Kerr polar-axis horizon `r₊_K = M + √(M²−a²)` at a finite
  `ε₃_crit(a)` that shrinks sharply with spin:
  `ε₃_crit(0.9) ≈ 0.1212`, `ε₃_crit(0.8) ≈ 0.4356`,
  `ε₃_crit(0.7) ≈ 1.0300`, and no transition in `[0, 3]` at a=0.5.
- For `ε₃ > ε₃_crit(a)`, no prograde photon orbit exists above the
  Kerr horizon. Retrograde orbit persists and is smooth across the
  entire swept range (`ε₃ ∈ [−1, +1]` at `a=0.9`).
- The transition is a BIFURCATION in the orbital structure, not a
  metric pathology (no CTCs on the equator, no signature change,
  1+h stays in `[0.32, 1.68]` across the approved sweep).
- Ray tracing remains well-posed for `ε₃ > ε₃_crit` — the image is
  not pathological; it is qualitatively different, with the
  prograde-side shadow bounded by the horizon rather than by a
  photon sphere. This is scientifically interesting and measurable
  (`δ_r/⟨r⟩` picks up the bifurcation).
- Phase 3C sweep at `a = 0.9` is a single 15-point curve spanning
  `[−2.5, +1.0]`, with a vertical line annotation at
  `ε₃_crit ≈ 0.12`. The cusp is the scientific headline (RNAAS
  reframe, see `CLAUDE.md` Project section): the bound on ε₃ is
  extracted from the smooth part of the curve, and the cusp is
  reported as a separate future-observable prediction.

### 2.3 Physical interpretation

The transition is the JP analogue of the Kerr extremality limit
`a → M`, where `r_photon_prograde → M`. In Kerr, spin up the hole
and the prograde photon orbit slides toward the horizon; here,
spin up ε₃ (at fixed a) and the photon orbit does the same. The
relevant control parameter is the Kerr margin
`Δr_K = r_photon_K(a) − r_plus_K(a)`; larger margin ⇒ more ε₃
needed to close the gap. Empirically, `ε₃_crit / Δr_K ≈ O(1)` —
consistent with the leading h correction on equator scaling as
`ε₃ M³/r³` and the photon-orbit condition linearizing to
`δr_photon ∝ ε₃ · r_photon⁻²` at small ε₃.

## 3. Investigation 2 — Johannsen 2013 bounds

**Scope caveat.** Direct retrieval of Johannsen 2013 (Phys. Rev. D
**88**, 044002) was not performed in this session; bounds below are
derived from the JP metric's mathematical properties and verified
numerically against `scripts/derive_jp_christoffels.py` and the
sweep script. Specific tabulated values from the paper (if any
tighter than those derived here) should be cross-referenced during
the Phase 3D manuscript pass. The following bounds are
conservative — any bound published in Johannsen 2013 that is
stricter supersedes them.

### 3.1 (a) Pathologies at `(a = 0.9, ε₃ = +0.5)`

Three pathologies to check:

**(i) `1 + h(r, θ) > 0` across the exterior.** Analytic argument
in `docs/phase-3-plan.md` §3.1 and numerically verified by the
grid scan in `scripts/derive_jp_christoffels.py`:
`1 + h ∈ [0.324, 1.676]` across `ε₃ ∈ [−2, +2]`, `a = 0.9 M`,
`r ≥ r₊_K`, `θ ∈ [0, π]`. **Holds at `(0.9, 0.5)`.** No signature
issue from this factor.

**(ii) Closed timelike curves (g_φφ < 0 outside the horizon).**
On the equator,
`g_φφ = (r² + a²) + (2Ma²r/Σ)(1+h) = (r² + a²) + (2Ma²/r)(1+h)`
for `r ≥ r₊_K` at `θ = π/2`. Both terms are positive when
`1 + h > 0`, which holds per (i). **No equatorial CTC at
`(0.9, 0.5)`.** Off-equator: `g_φφ = sin²θ · [(r² + a²) +
(2Ma²r sin²θ/Σ)(1+h)]`; same positivity argument applies with
`(1+h) > 0`, so the bracket is positive on the exterior, and
`sin²θ ≥ 0` makes `g_φφ ≥ 0`. **No CTC throughout the exterior
at `(0.9, 0.5)`** within the approved sweep range.

**(iii) Naked singularity (no closed horizon surface).** On the
polar axis `sin θ = 0`, `g_rr` diverges where `Δ = 0` →
`r = r₊_K = 1.4359 M`, independent of ε₃. So a polar-axis horizon
always exists at the Kerr location. Off-axis, the JP horizon is
the outermost root of `Δ + a² h sin²θ = 0`, which is θ-dependent
and can shift inward for `ε₃ > 0`. At `(0.9, 0.5)` on the equator,
`Δ + a² h = r² − 2r + 0.81 + 0.405/r³` is strictly positive for
all `r > 0` (minimum `≈ 0.08` at `r ≈ 1.25 M`). **So on the
equator, g_rr does not diverge** — the equatorial "horizon
surface" is absent; the horizon exists only as a θ-bounded cap
around the poles. This is a non-standard but not-pathological
horizon topology (cf. Chambers-Moss 1994 regular-black-hole
discussion for related topologies). The overall spacetime retains
an event horizon (topologically non-trivial) but with equatorial
coordinate structure that differs from Kerr. **Not a naked
singularity in the standard sense** but an atypical horizon
geometry.

**Summary of (a):** at `(0.9, +0.5)` the metric is regular on the
equator, has no CTCs, and retains a polar-axis horizon. The
equatorial horizon-surface structure is altered (the equatorial
`g_rr` no longer diverges), which is a non-standard feature but
not a pathology.

### 3.2 (b) Photon-sphere merger vs pathology boundary

The photon-sphere-merger transition at `ε₃_crit(a=0.9) ≈ 0.1212`
is distinct from the pathology boundaries of (i)–(iii) in §3.1:

- `1 + h > 0` is violated only at `ε₃ < −r₊³/M³ ≈ −2.96` at
  `a = 0.9` (equatorial-horizon approach).
- Equatorial CTC appearance requires `1 + h < 0` (same bound).
- Polar-axis horizon disappears only if `M² − a² < 0` (extremality),
  independent of ε₃.

All three pathology bounds are far from `ε₃ = +0.1212`. The
photon-sphere merger is a BIFURCATION in the orbital (causal)
structure at fixed spacetime geometry — the metric is smooth and
regular on both sides, but the circular null-orbit congruence
loses its prograde branch.

Analogy: in pure Kerr, `r_photon_prograde` monotonically drops
toward `M` as `a → M`. At `a = M` exactly, the prograde photon
orbit and the horizon coincide. Extremal Kerr is not pathological
(metric regular on the exterior), but the orbital structure is
degenerate. The JP transition is the same phenomenon shifted by
ε₃.

### 3.3 (c) Allowed physical range of ε₃ at a = 0.9

From the pathology analysis:

- `ε₃ > −2.96`: required for `1 + h > 0` on the equator at
  `r = r₊_K`. This is the binding lower bound (tightest of the
  three pathology checks).
- `ε₃` has no strict upper bound from pathology-avoidance on the
  equator up to at least `ε₃ = +2` (1+h stays in `[0.32, 1.68]`,
  per the grid scan).

**Derived physical range at a = 0.9: `ε₃ ∈ (−2.96, +∞)`** from the
pathology-avoidance perspective. The approved sweep range
`[−2, +2]` sits comfortably inside the pathology-free regime at
both ends.

**Caveat.** Johannsen 2013 may impose tighter bounds (e.g., from
off-equator CTC analysis, ergosphere-region regularity, or
regular-black-hole criteria). If the paper gives a tighter upper
bound, the derivation above is insufficient and the paper governs.

## 4. Investigation 3 — Image-plane implications

The user's three options:

- **(a) no bright ring** (shadow bounded by horizon or pathology alone)
- **(b) bright ring from a different orbital family**
- **(c) pathological image** (CTCs at horizon scale, ray tracing ill-posed)

**Answer: (b).** At `ε₃ > ε₃_crit(a)`:

- Retrograde photon orbit persists at `r ≈ 3.9 M` (a=0.9) — still
  generates a bright lensing ring from retrograde-impact-parameter
  photons.
- Prograde photon orbit has merged with the horizon — no trapping
  surface on that side. Rays approaching with prograde impact
  parameters plunge directly into the (topologically-complex)
  horizon region.
- Shadow boundary on the prograde side of the observer image is
  set by the HORIZON, not the photon sphere. On the retrograde
  side, still set by the photon sphere.
- Ray tracing is well-posed: `1+h > 0`, no CTCs, metric regular on
  the exterior (§3.1). The integrator produces a finite, physical
  image. Option (c) is ruled out.

### 4.1 Expected signature in δ_r/⟨r⟩

For `ε₃ < ε₃_crit`: smooth deformation of the Kerr shadow. Both
photon rings present (prograde + retrograde). `δ_r/⟨r⟩` varies
smoothly with ε₃, dominated by the competition between the two
ring radii.

For `ε₃ > ε₃_crit`: qualitatively different shadow — asymmetric
with one side bounded by the horizon (smaller effective radius on
that side) and the other by the retrograde photon ring (larger
radius). Asymmetry increases sharply across the transition. A
cusp or knee in `δ_r/⟨r⟩` vs ε₃ is expected near `ε₃_crit`.

### 4.2 Is the transition observable?

For the M87* 17° inclination and EHT angular resolution, the
transition is numerically distinct from smooth-deformation regime
by a detectable fraction of ⟨r⟩. Whether EHT's current uncertainty
on the Fourier m=1 amplitude (Paper VI Table 7) can resolve a
sweep through `ε₃_crit` is a quantitative question for Phase 3D.
Pre-emptively: at a=0.9, `ε₃_crit = 0.12` is well inside the
observationally-allowed region (EHT bounds are of order `|ε₃| < 1`
or wider, per Psaltis 2020), so the transition is not ruled out;
it is expected to FEATURE in the swept `δ_r(ε₃)` curve.

  the smooth-deformation regime

is therefore safe to extend to `ε₃_pathology ≈ −2.97` without
encountering any orbital bifurcation. Phase 3C stops at `−2.5`
(§5.2) with 0.47 M of safety margin.

### 7.2 Tight-bound grid scan at a = 0.9

Pathology test: `1 + h(r, θ) > 0` across
`r ∈ [r_plus_Kerr · 1.001, 5 M]` and `θ ∈ [0, π]`. Grid scan at
`ε₃ ∈ [−3.00, −2.80]` step 0.01 locates the minimum at
`(r, θ) = (1.4373, π/2)` — equator, just above the Kerr horizon —
for every ε₃ in that range. This confirms the §3.1 analytic claim
that the bound is attained at `(r₊_K, π/2)`.

Bisection on `min(1 + h)(ε₃) = 0`:

```
ε₃_pathology(a = 0.9) = −2.9695
```

The analytic single-point value `−r₊_K³ = −2.9606` agrees to 0.3%
relative; the small slack exists because the grid starts at
`r = r₊_K · 1.001` (r₊_K itself has Δ = 0 and cannot be sampled),
so the grid minimum sits at slightly larger r where |h| is
marginally smaller. **The findings-doc §3.3 lower bound `ε₃ > −2.97`
is tight to 0.3% and stands.** No revision needed to the sweep
range on the negative side beyond the `−2.5` safety margin approved
in §5.2.
